About Strategy Shepherds

Built for the expertise Africa already has.

African companies and leaders are sitting on decades of hard-won knowledge. Most of it never leaves the room. Strategy Shepherds exists to change that.

"Influence is the ultimate competitive advantage in the age of AI."

The Founder

Stella Njogo

Stella Njogo is a strategic communications expert focused on the Africa region, with experience working across African and global contexts.

She is a leading expert in how to build influence in a digital Africa. Through Strategy Shepherds, she works with organizations to review their strategy, sharpen their positioning, and build influence systems using proprietary frameworks she has developed and refined over time.

Stella has spent 12 years at the intersection of strategy, content, and African business. She has worked with companies, founders, and public figures across East and West Africa to build the kind of presence that opens doors before the pitch begins.

Strategy Shepherds grew out of a simple observation: African expertise is underrepresented online. Not because it does not exist. Because there has been no structured system to surface it, amplify it, and make it visible to the people who need to see it.

The result is that African companies consistently outperform their influence. They win contracts and lose narratives. They lead sectors and miss the conversation. Stella built Strategy Shepherds to solve exactly that problem.

Her clients have included Shujaaz Inc, Tiko Health, the Food Culture Alliance Kenya, and global institutions including TED Countdown and UN partners at UNGA. Results include influencing over $2 million in gender norms programming funding through research-led positioning, shaping a multi-year adolescent health fundraising narrative for Tiko across six African markets, and leading strategic communications work that travelled from Nairobi to TED Countdown and COP. The pattern is consistent: organisations that invested in influence stopped chasing opportunities and started attracting them.

Beyond client work, Stella publishes research on African digital markets and the creator economy through Digital Africa Signals on Substack, and releases weekly frameworks and field observations on YouTube.

The Problem We Solve

The internet is overwhelmingly Western. AI learns from what exists.

When African companies are absent from online conversations, they are absent from the models and algorithms that now shape global decision-making. That is not just a representation problem. It is a business problem.

Buyers do research before they buy. Partners Google before they commit. Investors look for signals before they move. If your company is not building visible authority, you are losing deals before the conversation starts.

The companies that build serious influence now will own the narrative for their sector. That is what we are here to help you do.

How We Work

We do not do branding. We build influence infrastructure.

There is a difference between looking credible and being the name people cite when decisions are being made. We focus on the latter.

01 / Diagnose

Start with the audit.

Every engagement begins with an honest read of where you currently stand. What is working, what is misdirected, and where the highest-leverage gaps are.

02 / Build

Co-create the roadmap.

We do not hand you a slide deck and disappear. We sit with you to build an influence strategy that fits your sector, your audience, and your resources.

03 / Compound

Make influence a system.

The goal is not one good piece of content. It is an authority system that produces results long after the engagement ends. Influence that compounds over time.
