The Gap

Good work that nobody can see is work that cannot compound.

By 2050, one in every four people on earth will be African. Yet the internet, the knowledge economy, and the emerging AI economy still do not sufficiently reflect African voices, African research, African nuance, or African problem-solving.

Across the continent, many organizations are doing excellent work. But too much of it is invisible, unpublished, under-amplified, or trapped in silos. People repeat work because they cannot see what already exists. Good ideas fail to compound. Strong institutions miss collaboration opportunities because their thinking is not discoverable.

This is not just a visibility problem. It is a prosperity problem.

In the current AI gold rush, everyone is racing to build the next tool, the next automation, the next platform. But when everyone has access to AI, tools alone will not make an organization stand out.

Influence will.

The organizations that win will be the ones people trust, cite, follow, invite, fund, buy from, and build with. Strategy Shepherds helps African organizations create that edge by turning their expertise, research, work, and ideas into influence that builds demand for their products, services, movements, and missions.

The Opportunity

Three levels of influence. One compounding system.

Influence is built in layers. Every service we offer maps to one of these three levels. Together, they build a system where each layer makes the next one stronger — and where your investment in one level pays dividends across all three.

About The Founder

Stella Njogo

Stella Njogo is a strategic communications expert focused on the Africa region, with experience working across African and global contexts.

She is a leading expert in how to build influence in a digital Africa. Through Strategy Shepherds, she works with organizations to review their strategy, sharpen their positioning, and build influence systems using proprietary frameworks she has developed and refined over time.

Stella has spent 12 years at the intersection of strategy, content, and African business, working with companies, founders, and public figures across East and West Africa to build the kind of presence that opens doors before the pitch begins.

Strategy Shepherds grew out of a simple observation: African expertise is underrepresented online. Not because it does not exist, but because there has been no structured system to surface it, amplify it, and make it visible to the people who need to see it.

Her clients have included Shujaaz Inc, Tiko Health, the Food Culture Alliance Kenya, and global institutions including TED Countdown and UN partners at UNGA. Results include influencing over $2 million in gender norms programming funding through research-led positioning, and shaping a multi-year adolescent health fundraising narrative for Tiko across six African markets.

Beyond client work, Stella publishes research on African digital markets and the creator economy through Digital Africa Signals on Substack, and releases weekly frameworks and field observations on YouTube.
