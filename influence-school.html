The Three Levels of Influence

Influence is built in layers. Your organisation needs to know which layer it is on.

These are not personality types or generic categories. They are infrastructure layers. Most organisations — including expert-led businesses, nonprofits, and SMEs — are operating below Level 2 without realising it. Understanding where your organisation is right now is the first step to building what actually moves the needle.

Level 1

Currently Open

Voice to Leads

Public method: Find Your Voice  ·  Strategic layer: Authority System

Your business becomes easier to understand, easier to trust and easier to buy from.

Most founders and SMEs are already visible online. Their website exists. They post occasionally. They promote their services. But their online presence is not consistently bringing in the right enquiries. The problem is clarity, not effort. People do not quickly understand what you do, who you help, why it matters, or what they should do next. Voice to Leads fixes that. It is the starting point for every business that wants its expertise to work harder online.

Join Level 1

Level 2

Coming Next

Community Trust

Public method: Gather Your People  ·  Strategic layer: Trust Network

You build deeper trust, stronger relationships, partnerships and community advantage.

Once your voice is clear and your lead pathway is working, the next layer is community. Strategic relationships, creator collaborations, built-in advocates and a network that carries your message into rooms you cannot enter alone. This level is not yet open.

Open to Level 1 graduates only.

Level 3

Advanced Level

Category Shaper

Public method: Enter and Shape the Conversation  ·  Strategic layer: Agenda Position

You become known for shaping the conversations that define how your market thinks, buys and decides.

The most powerful form of influence is not just being heard. It is being the one who sets the terms of the conversation. Category Shapers drive sector narratives, shape policy conversations and become the reference point their industry measures itself against. This is the advanced level. Details will be shared when Level 2 is open.
