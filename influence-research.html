Why We Do This

The creator economy is the most powerful influence infrastructure in the world. African businesses are largely absent from it.

Creators are not influencers in the old sense. They are trusted voices with loyal audiences that brands, institutions, and sectors spend years trying to build from scratch.

The businesses and sectors that learn to work with the creator economy gain something their competitors cannot buy. Distribution. Trust. Cultural relevance. All three are components of influence, and all three are sitting in the creator economy right now, waiting to be activated.

African sectors have been slow to see this. Strategy Shepherds exists to close that gap, starting with research that makes the opportunity impossible to ignore.

The Bigger Opportunity

Business and the creator economy grew up apart. That separation is now a business risk.

For most of the past decade, African businesses and African creators operated in parallel. Different conversations. Different networks. Different metrics of success.

That separation is closing fast. The companies that bridge it first will have a structural advantage that is very hard to replicate.

Strategy Shepherds is building that bridge. Through research that makes the case. Through partnerships that activate it.

Our Approach

Research that builds the case. Partnerships that activate it.

We work in two modes. First we research. Then we activate. The goal is not a campaign. It is a shift in how African sectors build influence, long term.

01

We Research

We document the creator economy landscape within specific African sectors, identify the gaps between where sector influence currently sits and where it could be, and publish findings that give decision-makers the evidence they need to act.

02

We Activate

We connect sectors to creator economy opportunities through structured partnerships. We work with the associations, programmes, and institutions that have the convening power to bring both sides together and the ambition to do something serious with it.

03

We Publish

Our findings and observations go into Digital Africa Signals, our Substack publication, and into the sector reports and thought pieces we produce for clients and partners.
