What Creator Day Is

The most powerful content is content other people make about you.

Think of it like a members' night, but for creators. Instead of your usual audience, you open your doors to local content creators — people with phones, platforms, and engaged followings — and you give them an experience worth sharing.

The creators come to discover something new. They get access to a space, a story, and a side of your business they would never have seen otherwise. In return, every creator who attends is asked to produce at least one piece of content — a post, a video, a photo — and tag your business.

No scripted testimonials. No production fees. No briefing an agency. Just real people discovering what you do and sharing it with the people who trust them.

Creator Day is a recurring event concept. The first one builds the template. The ones after it build momentum. The more you host, the more your space becomes a destination — and the more your influence in the local creator community compounds without spending a single dollar on advertising.

The Logic

Creators need new places to go. Your business is one of them.

Content creators are always looking for new experiences, new spaces, and new stories to tell their audiences. Most of them are cycling through the same cafes, co-working spaces, and restaurants every week.

A manufacturing floor, an energy company, a healthcare facility, a research lab, an agricultural processing plant — these are worlds creators have never entered. Which is exactly what makes them interesting.

Creator Day gives your business a way into that world. You host. They create. And every piece of content they publish sends their audience to discover you.

The more Creator Days you host, the more your space becomes a destination. The more your space becomes a destination, the more influence you build in your community — without spending a cent on advertising.

Real people. Real audiences. Real trust.

Creator content earns a different kind of trust than branded content. When someone's favorite creator visits your facility, tells their story, and shares what they experienced, that trust transfers. Paid ads cannot replicate it.

Your most interesting spaces deserve a wider audience.

Most businesses have stories that their customers never see. The behind-the-scenes. The production process. The people who make it happen. Creator Day surfaces those stories through the people best placed to tell them.

One Creator Day. Multiple pieces of organic content.

A single Creator Day typically produces content from multiple creators across multiple platforms. That is reach and presence you would have paid thousands to replicate through a brand content campaign.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions.

Do I need to have worked with creators before?

No. The toolkit and co-design session are specifically designed to walk you through the process from scratch. We help you design the experience, brief the creators, and set expectations so your first Creator Day runs smoothly.

How do creators find out about my Creator Day?

We promote your Creator Day on the Strategy Shepherds Creator Day Instagram page to our community of creators. You can also promote it through your own channels. We give you a creator-facing brief you can share widely.

How many creators typically attend?

This depends on your location, sector, and the experience you design. We help you plan for the right number during the co-design session. Smaller, more curated Creator Days often produce better content than large open events.

How often should I host a Creator Day?

There is no set requirement. Some businesses host once a quarter, others monthly. We recommend starting with one, reviewing the content that comes out of it, and deciding on your cadence from there. The more you host, the stronger the compounding effect.

What if a creator does not post?

We include a creator brief and attendance agreement in the toolkit that sets expectations clearly. Most creators are happy to post — it is content for their own channels. We help you set up the brief so participation feels natural rather than transactional.

What does the founding member rate mean?

We are building the Creator Day network and want to reward the organizations that join early. The first 10 Approved Creator Day Hosts pay $150 instead of $200 and become founding members of the network — with priority promotion as the network grows.
