What Agenda Shaping Is

You are already participating in the conversation. The question is whether you are doing it deliberately.

Every sector has a conversation happening around it. Debates about regulation, market shifts, emerging technologies, social issues, funding trends, consumer behavior, climate impact, geopolitical context. These conversations are shaping what gets funded, who gets hired, which organizations get invited to the table, and whose ideas get built.

Most organizations are passive participants. They read about what others are saying. They react to what gets published. They follow trends that are set by someone else.

Agenda shapers do not wait to be invited. They show up with something worth saying before the invitation arrives.

Agenda Shaping is the practice of deliberately engaging with the conversations that matter to your sector — not to be seen, but to contribute. To add a perspective that others cannot add. To surface research, data, or insight from your particular vantage point that changes how the conversation moves.

This is the highest form of influence because it is the hardest to replicate. You can copy a product. You can match a price. You cannot copy a perspective that comes from 10 years of deep work in a specific context.

That perspective is what Agenda Shaping turns into a strategic asset.

The Practice

Agenda Shaping has two parts. Most people only do one.

Effective agenda shaping is a deliberate cycle: you listen, you understand, and then you contribute — with intention and with something worth adding. Most organizations do one or the other, not both.

Part 01 · Conscious Consumption

Know what is being said before you say anything.

Agenda Shaping begins with active, deliberate attention to the conversations happening in your sector, your market, and the adjacent spaces that shape it. This means tracking sector publications, policy discussions, global frameworks, sector research, funding trends, and the perspectives being amplified by the people with institutional influence in your space.

The goal is not just to know what is happening. It is to understand where the gaps are, where the narrative is incomplete, and where your particular perspective — shaped by your geography, your sector experience, and your work — adds something that is not currently in the conversation.

Part 02 · Deliberate Contribution

Add your unique perspective to the conversation.

Once you know what is being said, the next step is deciding what only you can add. This is not about repeating what others are saying or creating content that echoes the mainstream. It is about identifying the insight, the data, the lived experience, or the counter-perspective that the conversation is missing — and contributing it through the channels where your audience is paying attention.

This could mean publishing research, contributing to policy consultations, speaking at global forums, writing op-eds in sector publications, creating a framework that others adopt, or building the infrastructure for a conversation that does not yet have a home.

What It Looks Like

Organizations that shape the agenda are recognized before they speak.

You know an agenda shaper when you encounter one. They are the ones who get invited to panels they did not apply for. They are cited in research they did not commission. They are included in briefings before decisions are made. Their ideas travel into rooms they have never entered.

Here is what agenda shaping looks like in practice:

  • An African health organization that does not just implement programs — but publishes the data that changes how funders allocate resources across the continent
  • A regional fintech company that participates in the global regulatory conversation and shapes how financial inclusion is defined in emerging markets
  • A founder whose LinkedIn posts on African supply chain challenges get cited by international logistics analysts
  • A nonprofit whose research on urban food systems in African cities gets picked up by UN bodies and informs global food security frameworks
  • A consulting firm that creates a framework for measuring organizational resilience in African markets that others in the sector start using as a standard

The common thread is not scale or budget. It is a willingness to invest in having a perspective, communicating it clearly, and showing up consistently in the spaces where it matters.
