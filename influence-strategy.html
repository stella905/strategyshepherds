How It Works

Four phases. Five weeks. One complete strategy.

The engagement runs fully remote. Stella's team works alongside yours, as collaborators.

Phase 1 · Week 1

Kickoff

One session. Stella and the commissioning team align on scope, priorities, and the right people to involve. You connect Stella's team with the stakeholders who will be interviewed during the audit.

This session establishes the influence hypothesis: what success looks like in your specific context. It produces the interview roster.

Phase 2 · Weeks 1–2

The Audit

Structured interviews with nominated stakeholders using Stella's proprietary Influence Infrastructure framework. The audit surfaces:

  • How expertise is actually perceived and carried versus how leadership believes it is
  • Authority gaps: what your organisation is known for versus what it should be known for
  • Trust network assumptions: who is actually carrying expertise forward
  • Absent agenda position: where decisions are being made without your voice

Phase 3 · Weeks 3–4

Co-Creation Week

One focused week building the strategy across all three layers with your commissioning team. This is collaborative strategy design, not a presentation of Stella's findings.

  • Authority: your distinctive point of view, made legible and AI-indexed
  • Trust Network: who carries your expertise forward and how that relationship is structured
  • Agenda Position: which conversations to enter, when, and how to shape them rather than join them

Phase 4 · Week 5

The Influence Mandate

Stella produces the complete strategy document and presents it to your top leadership: board, funders, or executive committee, whoever needs to authorise further investment.

The Mandate serves two purposes: a complete influence strategy your team owns and can activate immediately, and an evidence-backed internal investment brief that makes the case for what comes next.
